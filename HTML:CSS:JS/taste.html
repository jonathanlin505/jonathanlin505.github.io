WASHOKU

和食

NIGIRI
握り寿司

My favorite Japanese food, nigiri, is a type of sushi typically made of raw fish over rice. A good place to try nigiri and other Japanese seafood is Tsukiji Fish Market in Tokyo.

SUKIYAKI
すき焼き

A type of Japanese hot pot, sukiyaki is made up of a base of soy sauce, sugar, and mirin. Simply cook meat, vegetables, and or noodles inside and enjoy.

UNADON
鰻丼

Unadon is simply unagi (grilled eel), served over rice. Many restaurants in Japan specialize in unadon. I strongly recommend Unagi Komagata Maekawa in Tokyo.

MATCHA
抹茶

Despite its Chinese roots, matcha is almost idiosyncratic to Japan because of the deep-rooted tradition of chanoyu, the Japanese tea ceremony. If you're tired of shopping, visit a local teahouse for a nice cup of matcha or afternoon tea.

YOSHOKU

洋食

TONKATSU
豚カツ

Tonkatsu is a very popular Japanese dish. It is basically deep-fried, breaded pork cutlet. I highly recommend the Michelin-approved Tonkatsu Marugo in Akihabara.

YAKINIKU
焼き肉

If you enjoy Korean barbecue, you will definitely enjoy yakiniku, which basically translates to "grilled meat". If budget allows, you should try wagyu beef.

SOUFFLÉ PANCAKE
スフレパンケーキ

Japanese people are no strangers to desserts. Soufflé pancakes are very popular among the younger generations. If you have the chance, try this dish at Bills in Tokyo.

BAUMKUCHEN
バームクーヘン

You can't leave Japan without gifts for family and friends. A good gift is the baumkuchen, one of the most popular pastries in Japan. Nenrinya sells the best ones in my opinion. If you do go there, get the ones with the rough edges like the ones in the picture.
