JAPAN
日本

The cities you've decided to visit are like the sun and the moon.
While Tokyo boasts countless high-rises and modern architecture, Kyoto humbles in its historic temples and deeply rooted traditions.

TOKYO
KYOTO

TOKYO

東京

SHIBUYA
渋谷

Videos of crowds of Japanese people crossing a big intersection? Yeah, that's probably Shibuya. It is the biggest commercial and business center in Japan as well as a fashion (e.g. Harajuku style) district and a popular spot for nightlife.

GINZA
銀座

Another major commercial district, Ginza is the equivalent of Beverley Hills in Los Angeles. This place is packed with high-end boutiques, department stores, and restaurants.

AKIHABARA
秋葉原

If you like anime, video games, and or cheap electronics, this is the place to be. Akihabara caters specifically towards Otaku culture. For such reason, you may find large electronics department stores, maid cafés, and weird Otaku stuff that creeps me out.

SHINJUKU
新宿

Directly north of Shibuya is Shinjuku. Known as the "Sleepless Town", it is the home of the world-famous red-light district, Kabukicho. This is the go-to place to immerse yourself in the weird and wonderful Japanese nightlife.

KYOTO

京都

KINKAKU-JI
金閣寺

Also known as the "Golden Pavillion" due to its decadent use of gold-leaf coating, Kinkaku-ji is a Zen Buddhist temple constructed in 1397. This is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Kyoto as well as a World Heritage Site.

FUSHIMI INARI TAISHA
伏見稲荷大社

Fushimi Inari Taisha is an important Shinto shrine famous for its endless rows of torii gates, which are donated by people since the Edo period to be granted a wish or to thank for a granted wish. The main path consists of around 1,000 torii gates.

GION
祇園

Gion is the geisha district of Kyoto so it is not uncommon to see people walking around in colorful kimonos. It is a nice place to experience Japanese traditions such as the tea ceremony and the Kyomai dances. Near Gion, there are plentiful temples with beautiful Zen gardens to visit.

ARASHIYAMA
嵐山

Arashiyama is filled with temples just like the rest of Kyoto, but it is most known for its scenic bamboo forest. Speaking of bamboo, I recommend the movie, Tale of Princess Kaguya  by Studio Ghibli, based on the classic Japanese folklore, Tale of the Bamboo Cutter.
